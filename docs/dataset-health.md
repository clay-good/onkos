## Practical identifiability by model

A model flagged *not identifiable* has a parameter whose predicted relative standard error exceeds 50% (or a collinear, non-separable parameter combination) — so its reported point value, and often the large IIV CV travelling with it, are partly a flat-likelihood artifact of the design rather than clean estimates. These are the models where a richer trial design or an external constraint is needed before an estimate should be reused (curation triage). Design level only; identifiability cannot move a tier.
