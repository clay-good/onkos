# Onkos — research spec: acquired (drug-induced) resistance — the resistance *origin* as a model-selection axis

**Status:** implemented in v0.32.0 (`acquired_resistance` kernel + record). This is the
design-of-record; the methodological source anchors of §7 are documented but their
Crossref-verified citation curation is still pending, honest by design. Written in the v0.1
house style; every value is illustrative and `unverified` by design — the infrastructure is the
contribution.

**Two tumors can carry resistance for opposite reasons and look identical at week 8.** v0.24
added the two-population (Goldie-Coldman) model and made the resistance *mechanism* a
model-selection axis — phenomenological decay-of-effect (Claret) vs a mechanistic resistant
subclone. But that mechanistic model encodes one specific *origin* of resistance: a **pre-existing**
resistant clone, present at baseline (`R0 > 0`), that the drug cannot touch. The other canonical
origin is **acquired** resistance: no resistant cell exists at baseline, but under drug pressure
sensitive cells *convert* to resistant at a switching rate `α` — resistance is *generated by the
treatment itself*. This spec adds that model and shows the resistance **origin** — acquired vs
pre-existing — is a model-selection axis one layer below v0.24's mechanism axis, and one the
week-8 survival surrogate cannot see.

> The clinical distinction is real and consequential. Pre-existing resistance argues for hitting
> hard and early (kill the sensitive bulk before the resistant clone dominates); acquired
> resistance argues that *more drug generates more resistance* (the switching flux is
> drug-driven), so the optimal schedule is different. Onkos does not pick a side — it makes the
> two origins two explicit, comparable models and shows that, matched on every shared parameter,
> they agree at week 8 and on the week-8-driven OS surrogate yet diverge in the regrowth tail.
> The origin is a silent assumption baked into a resistance model; Onkos makes it visible.

---

## 1. The problem this extends

The two-population model (v0.24) is `dS/dt = (kg − kd·E)·S`, `dR/dt = kgr·R`, with the resistant
clone seeded by a **pre-existing** burden `R0 > 0`. That `R0` is an assumption — it asserts the
resistance was there all along. The acquired-resistance model keeps the *same* sensitive and
resistant kinetics but changes the origin:

```
dS/dt = (kg − kd·E)·S − α·E·S        sensitive: grow, killed by E, AND switch out under drug
dR/dt =  kgr·R        + α·E·S        resistant: grow + the drug-induced acquired influx
R(0)  = R0 = 0                        no resistance pre-exists; the pool is generated by treatment
```

The single new parameter is `α`, the **drug-induced switching rate** (the Goldie-Coldman
acquired-mutation kinetics). Setting `α = 0, R0 > 0` recovers the pre-existing two-population
model exactly; setting `R0 = 0, α > 0` is the pure acquired model. With `kg`, `kd`, `kgr`
**matched to the pre-existing model**, the *only* difference is the resistance origin — so any
divergence in the prediction is attributable to the origin assumption alone, exactly as v0.24
matched `kd` to Claret to isolate the mechanism.

**Why this is the right deepening (and the right scope).** It (1) extends the v0.24 mechanism
axis to the resistance **origin**, the next silent assumption down — the reserve list's
"acquired-resistance switching rate (v0.24 follow-on)"; (2) is a first-class **new kernel** (like
Norton-Simon v0.18 and two-population v0.24), not post-processing, so it slots into the *existing*
compare / divergence / PFS-route / budget machinery for free; (3) is standard and named —
acquired vs pre-existing resistance is the Goldie-Coldman distinction, with Foo-Michor (2014),
*Evolution of acquired resistance to anti-cancer therapy*, as the apt primary citation; (4) is
*safe by construction* — a population trajectory over a published model structure, no patient
data; (5) reinforces the load-bearing message: matched on everything else, the origin choice is
invisible to the week-8 OS surrogate but visible in the tail — another silent-transport risk made
measurable.

---

## 2. The result — same week-8, different tail

For NSCLC first line at unit drug effect, the acquired model (`α = 0.02`, `R0 = 0`) against the
pre-existing two-population model (`R0 = 1.5`, `α = 0`), matched on `kg`/`kd`/`kgr` (illustrative):

| Quantity | Acquired (switching) | Pre-existing (subclone) | sees the origin? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Week-8 tumor size | 13 mm | 10 mm | barely — both ~deep responders |
| Median OS (week-8 link) | **92 wk** | **94 wk** | **no** — the surrogate is blind |
| Median PFS (week-8 link) | 36 wk | 37 wk | no |
| Nadir depth | **8.0 mm** | **2.8 mm** | **yes** — acquired is shallower |
| Nadir time | 16 wk | 21 wk | yes — acquired is earlier |
| Mechanistic PFS (RECIST TTP) | **26 wk** | **32 wk** | **yes** — acquired progresses earlier |

Read the two halves. **At week 8 and on the week-8-driven OS/PFS surrogate, the two origins are
nearly identical** (OS 92 vs 94 wk) — a trial reading out at week 8 cannot tell an acquired-
resistance tumor from a pre-existing-resistance one. **But the mechanism diverges in the tail:**
the acquired model has a markedly *shallower* nadir (8.0 vs 2.8 mm) — because the drug that kills
the sensitive clone simultaneously *generates* the resistant one, so the tumor never shrinks as
deeply — and it reaches RECIST progression *earlier* (26 vs 32 wk). The resistance origin is a
real divergence the week-8 surrogate misses and the **mechanistic PFS (v0.30) catches**. Adding
the acquired model to NSCLC raises the PFS route-discordant pairs (mechanistic vs statistical) to
4 of 10.

**The honest framing.** Onkos does not claim either origin is correct — both are illustrative,
tier-C, `unverified`. It claims the **origin is a choice** with consequences a week-8 readout
cannot see, so a resistance model that silently assumes one origin (as every single-mechanism
model does) carries an unquantified model-selection risk. The contrast makes it quantified.

## 3. Tier & guardrail propagation (unchanged invariants)

- **Worst-input-wins governs.** The acquired model carries its propagated tier (C); out-of-context
  transport floors to D with a warning, exactly as for the two-population model. Its
  `known_failure_mode` — applied where resistance is pre-existing not acquired — floors to D.
- **Population / trial level only.** Trajectories and population OS/PFS, never an individual.
- **No therapy ranking.** The acquired-vs-pre-existing comparison ranks *models under a context*,
  never treatments or schedules; the clinical aside in the header is context, not a recommendation.
- **`α` is honestly low-identifiability.** Like the pre-existing `R0`, the switching rate carries a
  ~100%+ IIV CV and is flagged as poorly identified from a short trial (it is the acquired analog of
  `R0`, and `onkos.identify` / `onkos.design` treat it the same way).

## 4. Validation landmarks

The kernel is round-trip validated like every ODE record (SBML MathML, NONMEM `$THETA`,
rxode2/Pumas params — added to `ODE_RECORDS`). The dynamics suite (`tests/test_acquired.py`) pins
the behavior:

| Landmark | Condition |
| --- | --- |
| **Recovers two-population at α=0** | with `α = 0` the acquired kernel's trajectory equals the two-population kernel's (same `kg`/`kd`/`kgr`/`R0`) — the strict-generalization check. |
| **Conservation / influx sign** | the acquired influx `α·E·S` leaves `S` and enters `R` one-for-one; with `R0 = 0` and `α > 0` the resistant pool is strictly generated (R(t) > 0 for t > 0 under drug). |
| **Shallower nadir than pre-existing** | matched on `kg`/`kd`/`kgr`, the acquired model's nadir is *less deep* than the pre-existing model's (drug-driven switching limits the depth of response). |
| **Earlier RECIST progression** | the acquired model's mechanistic time-to-progression (v0.30) is *shorter* than the pre-existing model's. |
| **Week-8 OS agreement** | the two origins' median OS under the week-8 link agree within a small tolerance — the surrogate is blind to the origin. |
| **No drug ⇒ no acquired resistance** | with `E = 0` the switching flux vanishes (`α·E·S = 0`); the tumor is pure sensitive-clone growth. |
| **Tier & guardrails** | the propagated tier rides through; out-of-context transport floors to D; the clinical-use prohibition is on every export. |

## 5. API, CLI, and surface

The acquired model is an ordinary TGI record — every existing surface works on it unchanged:

```python
# Forward simulation and the existing virtual-trial divergence view.
tr = onkos.simulate(ds, "resistance.nsclc_first_line.acquired", context=ctx, drug_effect=1.0)
cmp = onkos.compare(ds, purpose="tgi", context=ctx)        # now includes the acquired model

# The origin divergence is read by the existing PFS-route and response machinery.
pf = onkos.progression_free_survival(ds, "resistance.nsclc_first_line.acquired", context=ctx)
pf.median_ttp_weeks          # ~26 — earlier than the pre-existing model's ~32

# It binds a new ODE kernel, so it exports like any other.
onkos.export ...             # SBML / NONMEM / PharmML / rxode2 / Pumas (round-trip validated)
```

**No new module or CLI command** — the contribution is a kernel + a record + the finding, surfaced
through the existing `simulate` / `compare` / `pfs` / `response` / export paths and a figure.

## 6. Source anchors (methodological; DOIs added at curation time)

- **Acquired-resistance evolution.** Foo, J. & Michor, F. (2014), *Evolution of acquired resistance
  to anti-cancer therapy*, J. Theor. Biol. 355:10–20 (DOI 10.1016/j.jtbi.2014.02.025) — the
  drug-induced switching kinetics this model encodes (the primary citation).
- **Goldie-Coldman hypothesis.** Goldie, J.H. & Coldman, A.J. (1979) — the foundational
  acquired-mutation / resistant-clone framework (conceptual; pre-DOI), the basis for treating the
  switching rate as the acquired analog of the pre-existing resistant fraction.
- **Tumor-dynamics / resistance review.** Bruno et al. (2020) — the basis for the
  `known_failure_mode` (an acquired model applied where resistance is pre-existing fabricates the
  tail).

## 7. Deliberate non-goals (so the scope stays honest)

- **Not a fitted switching rate.** `α` is a declared, illustrative parameter, never estimated from
  data here; like `R0` it is honestly flagged as practically unidentifiable from a short trial.
- **Not a combined origin model (yet).** The shipped record is the *pure* acquired model
  (`R0 = 0, α > 0`); a mixed model (`R0 > 0, α > 0`) is a clean superset the kernel already
  supports (it is the union of both terms) but is left as a follow-on.
- **Not cross-context (yet).** The introduction adds the kernel and one NSCLC record (mirroring
  v0.24's single-context introduction of the two-population model, broadened later in v0.29); the
  4 other solid-tumor contexts are a clean breadth follow-on.
- **No schedule optimization here.** The clinical aside (acquired resistance favors a different
  schedule) is not acted on — that is `onkos.design` territory, and Onkos never recommends a
  schedule.

---

## 8. Safety & scope (unchanged hard line)

- **Population / trial level only.** Trajectories and population OS/PFS over a published model
  structure; nothing is an individual prediction.
- **No therapy ranking, no scheduling.** The origin comparison ranks models, never treatments.
- **Cannot raise a tier.** The acquired model is tier C; out-of-context transport floors to D.
- **The line, restated.** Any feature that takes a real patient's data and returns a resistance
  diagnosis, a prognosis, or a treatment/schedule choice **does not get built.** Making the
  resistance-origin assumption explicit and its tail consequences computable changes none of this.
